## Option A: Take Your Own Screenshots (RECOMMENDED)

### If You Own the Games:

**Hollow Knight (8 screenshots):**
1. **Download/Own the game** (~$15 on Steam/Switch, often on sale)
2. **How to screenshot:**
   - **Steam:** Press F12 (screenshots save to Steam folder)
   - **Windows:** Press Windows+G for Game Bar, then capture
   - **Mac:** Command+Shift+3
3. **What to capture:**
   - 3 navigation scenes: caves, platforms, different areas
   - 3 combat scenes: with different enemy types
   - 2 NPC scenes: talking to characters

**Stardew Valley (7 screenshots):**
1. **Download/Own the game** (~$15, often on sale)
2. **Screenshot method:** Same as above
3. **What to capture:**
   - 3 farm scenes: crops, animals, tools visible
   - 2 town scenes: buildings, paths, NPCs
   - 2 indoor scenes: inside house or Pierre's store

**Where screenshots are saved:**
- **Steam:** `C:\Program Files (x86)\Steam\userdata\[your_id]\760\remote\[game_id]\screenshots`
- **Windows Game Bar:** `C:\Users\[YourName]\Videos\Captures`
- **Mac:** Desktop or Pictures folder

# CATEGORY 2: INDOOR NAVIGATION (15 images)
**Responsible:** Roshan
**Time:** 1-2 hours

## Option A: Take Your Own Photos (RECOMMENDED)

### What You Need:
- Smartphone camera
- 30-45 minutes
- Access to various indoor spaces

### Locations to Photograph:

**Hallways (4 images):**
1. Empty hallway - straight view
2. Hallway with people walking
3. Hallway with obstacles (chairs, trash cans)
4. Hallway corner/turn

**Where:** Your apartment building, campus building, library

**Doorways (3 images):**
1. Open door - view into room
2. Closed door - showing handle
3. Double doors (like building entrance)

**Where:** Dorm, classroom building, library

**Stairs (3 images):**
1. Stairs going up - from bottom
2. Stairs going down - from top
3. Staircase with railing visible

**Where:** Any multi-story building

**Rooms (5 images):**
1. Living room - with furniture
2. Kitchen - with appliances visible
3. Bedroom - with bed, desk
4. Classroom - desks, board
5. Office/study area

**Where:** Your home, friend's place (with permission), campus spaces

### Photography Tips:
- **Hold phone at chest/shoulder height** (simulates blind person using camera)
- **Take during daytime** for good lighting
- **Include variety:** empty vs. crowded, clean vs. cluttered
- **Landscape orientation** (horizontal)
- **Don't include people's faces** (privacy)

**File naming:**
```
indoor_hallway_empty_01.png
indoor_hallway_obstacle_01.png
indoor_door_open_01.png
indoor_stairs_up_01.png
indoor_room_living_01.png
```

## Option B: Use Stock Photos (FREE, LEGAL)

### Best Free Stock Photo Sites:

**Unsplash:** https://unsplash.com/
- Search: "hallway", "stairs", "indoor", "doorway"
- **License:** Free to use, no attribution required
- **Quality:** Excellent, high-resolution

**Pexels:** https://www.pexels.com/
- Search: "corridor", "building interior", "staircase"
- **License:** Free for research use
- **Quality:** Very good

**Pixabay:** https://pixabay.com/
- Search: "indoor navigation", "building interior"
- **License:** Free, but check individual images
- **Quality:** Good variety

### How to Download from Stock Sites:
1. Search for your category (e.g., "hallway")
2. Click on image you like
3. Click "Download" or "Free Download"
4. Choose size (Large or Original)
5. Save to `data/images/indoor/` folder

### Recommended Searches:
- "empty hallway"
- "office corridor"
- "apartment hallway"
- "staircase interior"
- "open doorway"
- "room interior"
- "kitchen view"
- "living room"

**Pros:** Professional quality, legally clear, fast
**Cons:** May look too polished (not realistic for accessibility testing)

# CATEGORY 3: OUTDOOR NAVIGATION (10 images)
**Responsible:** Dhesel
**Time:** 1-2 hours

## Option A: Take Your Own Photos (RECOMMENDED)

### What to Photograph:

**Crosswalks/Streets (3 images):**
1. Crosswalk - straight view from sidewalk
2. Street corner - showing both directions
3. Intersection - with traffic lights visible

**Where:** Any street near campus or home
**Safety tip:** Don't stand in the road! Shoot from sidewalk

**Sidewalks (2 images):**
1. Clear sidewalk - no obstacles
2. Sidewalk with obstacles - bikes, posts, people

**Where:** Campus walkways, city sidewalks

**Building Entrances (2 images):**
1. Building entrance - showing door and steps if any
2. Store entrance - with signage visible

**Where:** Academic buildings, local shops

**Outdoor Stairs/Ramps (2 images):**
1. Outdoor stairs - going up/down
2. Wheelchair ramp

**Where:** Campus buildings, public spaces

**Parking Lot/Open Area (1 image):**
1. Parking lot or plaza - showing open space and cars

**Where:** Campus parking, shopping center

### Photography Tips:
- **Daytime only** - need good lighting for CV models
- **Include varied weather:** sunny, cloudy (if possible)
- **Avoid rainy/dark conditions** (makes ground truth labeling hard)
- **Chest height** - realistic camera position
- **Landscape orientation**
- **Blur faces** if people are present (or avoid faces)

**File naming:**
```
outdoor_crosswalk_01.png

outdoor_entrance_01.png

# CATEGORY 4: TEXT/SIGN READING (10 images)
**Responsible:** Dhesel
**Time:** 1 hour

## Option A: Take Your Own Photos (EASIEST!)

### What to Photograph:

**Street Signs (3 images):**
1. Street name sign
2. Stop sign or traffic sign
3. Directional sign (campus building signs)

**Where:** Anywhere outdoors

**Store Signs (2 images):**
1. Store front sign (e.g., "Starbucks", "CVS")
2. Business sign with text

**Where:** Local shopping area

**Product Labels (2 images):**
1. Food label - nutrition facts visible
2. Product packaging with text

**Where:** Your kitchen, grocery store (ask permission), pharmacy

**Menus (2 images):**
1. Restaurant menu - paper or board
2. Cafe menu - price list visible

**Where:** Local restaurant/cafe (ask permission to photograph)

**Informational Signs (1 image):**
1. Building directory, hours sign, or warning sign

**Where:** Campus buildings, public spaces

### Photography Tips:
- **Close enough to read text** - text should be clear
- **Good lighting** - essential for OCR
- **Straight angle** - not too skewed
- **Focus on text** - text should be sharp
- **High resolution** - don't zoom in too much

**File naming:**
```
text_sign_street_01.png
text_sign_store_01.png
text_label_food_01.png
text_menu_restaurant_01.png
text_info_hours_01.png

## Image Quality Requirements:

**Resolution:**
- Minimum: 640x480 (VGA)
- Recommended: 1280x720 (HD)
- Ideal: 1920x1080 (Full HD)

**Format:**
- Save as PNG (best quality, no compression)
- Or JPG with quality >90%

**Lighting:**
- Well-lit scenes (avoid dark/night photos)
- No extreme overexposure
- Natural or indoor lighting OK

**Focus:**
- Sharp, not blurry
- Main subjects in focus
- OK if background slightly blurred
